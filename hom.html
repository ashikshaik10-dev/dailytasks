Massachusetts Institute of Technology

The Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) is a private research university in Cambridge, Massachusetts, known for science, engineering, computing, architecture, economics, and entrepreneurship. Founded in 1861, it has become one of the world's most influential research institutions, with a strong record of technological innovation, Nobel laureates, and startup creation.

Academic strengths

MIT is organized into several schools and a college covering fields from engineering and computer science to business, humanities, and architecture. Its educational approach emphasizes hands-on learning, interdisciplinary collaboration, and research opportunities that often begin at the undergraduate level.
